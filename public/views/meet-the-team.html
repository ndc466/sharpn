Alex Makris
Director

Alex Makris is Vice President in the Houston Brokerage Services Team of CBRE specializing in the purchase and sale of land. Alex has experience in asset disposition, land sales, investment sales, retail and residential development, as well as project leasing. Alex has represented investors and developers such as Trammell Crow Company, L&B Realty Advisors, Shanda Worldwide, Cole Capital, Inland Western, Credit Suisse, KB Homes, Rooted Development, The Finger Companies, David Weekley Homes, HEB Stores and Toll Brothers. He also has experience negotiating with national retail tenants such us Walmart, Academy, Bed Bath & Beyond, Ross, TJX Company, Michaels, Best Buy, Kroger and many others. Alex came to CBRE through the Trammell Crow merger and has been with the combined companies for over fifteen years. Alex studied at the University of Houston and enjoyed a successful 12-year career in the restaurant business in Houston with Taste of Texas Restaurant and Pappas Restaurants prior to joining CBRE. In his spare time, Alex enjoys running, coaching various sports and spending time with his wife and four children.

Ryan Homeyer
Director

Ryan Homeyer is an associate in the Houston office of Locke Lord LLP where he practices in the Firm's Private Wealth and Tax sections. Ryan focuses on estate and wealth transfer planning, probate and estate administration, asset protection planning, philanthropic planning, and marital property protection planning. Ryan also advises clients regarding basic testamentary and incapacity planning documents.Prior to joining the Firm’s Private Wealth and Tax sections, Ryan practiced for two years in the Firm’s Business Litigation and Dispute Resolution group, gaining valuable experience assisting clients in commercial contract disputes, assisting subcontractors, general contractors, and owners in construction disputes, advising clients on contractual risks, managing discovery challenges, and drafting and defending dispositive motions.Ryan Homeyer graduated from Texas A&M University, where he earned a degree in Industrial and Systems Engineering, after which he earned his Juris Doctor degree from Washington University in St. Louis. While at Texas A&M, Ryan served as a co-chair of Fish Camp, Texas A&M's extended freshman orientation program (and the largest student-run on campus organization in the nation). As a co-chair, Ryan was in charge of developing 24 counselors in order to successfully introduce over 120 freshman to campus, Texas A&M traditions, and college life. While in law school, Ryan was Editor for the Washington University Law Review. Ryan is also an active member of Houston's First Baptist Church, where he serves as Director of the Kaleo Life Bible Study Class. As Director, Ryan oversees a team of core leadership. Alongside a team of core leadership, Ryan started the Kaleo Life Bible Study Class and through God's provision has watched the class grow to over 75 active members over the past ten months.
